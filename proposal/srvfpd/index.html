The opening you handed us

Two inbound conversions in a 24-hour window, from your Fire Chief and your Medical Director, is a clear and well-aligned signal. We took the meeting seriously. Mike Guadan and Dr. Johnson spoke by phone the day after Dr. Johnson's form arrived, and the working theme that emerged was simple: before either side talks about a partnership, let's look at the data together.

This proposal does not ask SRVFPD to make a vendor decision. It asks for a structured 12 to 24 month working engagement, under NDA, to study your determinant-code-level call data and surface where a non-911 care pathway would meaningfully improve outcomes, reduce truck-hours, and align low-acuity callers with the right setting. We bring the analysis, the public-safety domain expertise, and the comparative benchmarks. You bring the data and the operational context. The output is yours.

The Engagement

12-24 Month Master Dispatch Analysis
Determinant-code-level review under mutual NDA · Joint deliverables · Renewable
Determinant-code segmentation
EMD chief complaint and determinant code distribution across 12-24 months. Identifies the low-acuity tail and the Alpha-Omega routable cohort.
Truck-hour economics
Cost of readiness mapped against utilization. Quantifies the readiness preserved by removing non-emergent transports from the response queue.
Disposition modeling
Maps each routable cohort to the right alternative setting: clinician-on-camera, Omega-ambulance, rideshare, crisis line, poison control, law non-emergency.
Comparative benchmarking
SRVFPD performance side-by-side with comparable CA agencies (Falck/Alameda, peer fire-EMS districts). No PII shared, no agency identified.
Investment scope: to be defined jointly during the working session and codified in the engagement agreement. NDA is mutual and pre-signed before any data exchange.
